The Song of Polaris

is a tabletop RPG system I built from the ground up as an alternative to the D&D 5th edition framework, and I'm currently running a campaign using it! I wanted to create a gameplay space for my players to have full-spectrum customization over their characters, with significantly less downtime during combat, and more opportunities for meaningful free-form roleplay. The story takes place in a magical, post-apocalyptic world faced with the threat of annihilation by an elder Greatsoul, and the players are rushing to fulfill a prophecy gone sideways.

This was my first time writing a full ruleset, and I drew heavy inspiration from the D&D 5e core books, Starfinder, Pok√©mon games, and other roleplay systems I've encountered or used in the past. As we play, I am constantly editing, fine-tuning, and fixing the rulebook, but the most recent version can be found here! Feel free to take a look.


Balatro: En Carde

is a prototype player-versus-player tabletop edition of the indie roguelike game Balatro. Originally coined as "joker poker", Balatro is a "poker-inspired roguelike deck builder all about creating powerful synergies and winning big".

Balatro: En Carde takes it one step further, wrapping the game around back to a competitive card game, keeping the additions of Balatro's powerful Jokers, Tarot Cards, and newly possible hands. Beginning with a standard deck of cards each (and a reserve deck to spare) players must simultaneously craft the best poker hand to win each Ante, utilizing and competing over advantageous Jokers and Tarot Cards.

You can read the rules here and play with your friends and family! Do note that the game is a prototype and has not been blind playtested, so there may be gaps in the rules that are yet to be accounted for. The Balatro property is owned by Playstack and LocalThunk.

Hades: The Darkride

is a two part paper proposal and simple state simulator for a theoretical amusement part attraction based on the Hades games (I & II). The ride is designed to create a highly immersive themed journey for riders using cutting edge technology; with an end result close to something like Disney's Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance.

The paper proposal can be found here; it contains a link to the simulation.

CMPM147: Generative Design Portfolio

is a secondary portfolio that showcases my work during my time learning generative design. The class covered generative design both in games and art, but I focused heavily on world/level generation. My biggest takeaways from the class were learning how to mix and layer noise, and the different styles programmers use to generate complex and emergent buildings, structures, level layouts, and decorations.

Out of the projects I worked on in that time, my best were by far project 5, the image annealer, and Gem-etic Dungeon, the final project. Gem-etic Dungeon can also be found on my itch.io page.
